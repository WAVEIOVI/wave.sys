### Commission Calculation Examples

**Example 1: New Equipment Sale**
- Customer orders 20 fire extinguishers + installation
- Total invoice: 15,000 TND
- Equipment (12,000 TND) √ó 4% = 480 TND
- Installation (3,000 TND) √ó 3% = 90 TND

- **When Paid:** When customer pays invoice (if paid in 30 days, commission paid in Month 2)

**Example 2: Annual Maintenance Contract (3-Year)**

- Commission: 10,000 √ó 6% = 600 TND (on first year value)

  * If customer pays annually in advance: 600 TND when payment received

### Commission Payment & Administration

- **Critical rule:** Commission paid when customer pays (not when sale is made)

- Commission clawbacks: If product is returned or service cancelled, commissions are reversed and recovered from future payments

## 4.3 SICK LEAVE

**Entitlement (Per Tunisian Labor Code):**

**With Medical Certificate:**
- Employee can take sick leave when genuinely ill
- Must provide medical certificate (from a licensed doctor)
- **Duration:** Up to 6 months for the same illness
- **Payment:**
  * **First 30 days:** 50% of salary for the first month (per labor code; may be up to 100% depending on CNSS rules and circumstances)
  * **Days 31‚Äì180:** 66% of salary (paid by CNSS; employer may supplement)
  * **After 180 days:** CNSS disability benefits apply

**SS+ Policy:**
- **Full salary is maintained for the first 3 months** of sick leave (company supplements CNSS where required)
- Long-term illness handled on a case-by-case, compassionate basis

**Without Medical Certificate:**
- Sick leave is **not paid** (considered unauthorized absence)
- **Exception:** 1‚Äì2 day minor illness at manager's discretion (maximum 2 times per year)

**Procedure:**
1. **Notify immediately** ‚Äî Call your manager before the start of your shift (do not text or message where a call is possible). Explain the nature of the illness and expected absence.
2. **See a doctor** and obtain a medical certificate ‚Äî Must be issued by a licensed doctor and obtained **within 48 hours** of the absence start; certificate should state diagnosis, recommended rest period, and include the doctor's stamp and signature.
3. **Submit the certificate** ‚Äî Provide it to the Admin Responsible (in person, by photo via WhatsApp/email); submit the original when you return to work.
4. **Return to work** ‚Äî On the date specified in the medical certificate (or sooner if recovered). If an extension is needed, obtain and submit a new certificate before the current one expires.

**Abuse of Sick Leave:**
- Suspected abuse (excessive absences, suspicious patterns) will be investigated. The company may require an examination by a company-designated doctor and CNSS may be involved; proven abuse can lead to disciplinary action up to termination.

**CNSS Work Incapacity Benefits:**
- For serious illness or injury preventing work, CNSS provides financial support; Admin Responsible will assist with any CNSS claim process.

## 7.3 DISCIPLINE PROCESS

**Our Approach - Fair and Progressive:**

We believe in giving people opportunities to improve. If performance or behavior issues arise, we use progressive discipline (escalating steps). Purpose is to correct behavior, not punish.

**Progressive Discipline Steps:**

**STEP 1: Verbal Coaching (Informal)**
- **When:** First minor infraction or performance issue
- **Process:** Manager speaks with you privately, clarifies expectations, offers support
- **Documentation:** Manager keeps informal notes
- **Goal:** Quick correction before becomes pattern

**STEP 2: Verbal Warning (Formal)**
- **When:** Repeat of issue after coaching, OR moderate infraction
- **Process:** Formal meeting, documented, clear expectations set, consequences explained
- **Documentation:** Verbal warning form signed by both, in your personnel file
- **Goal:** Make clear this is serious, improvement required

**STEP 3: Written Warning**
- **When:** Repeat after verbal warning, OR serious infraction first time
- **Process:** Written warning letter detailing issue, history, expectations, timeline for improvement
- **Documentation:** Signed by both, copy to you, in personnel file
- **Consequences:** Further issues may lead to termination
- **Goal:** Final wake-up call, last chance to improve

**STEP 4: Final Written Warning / Suspension**
- **When:** Repeat after written warning, OR very serious infraction
- **Process:** Final warning issued, may include unpaid suspension (1-5 days)
- **Message:** This is your absolute last chance
- **Next Step:** Any further infraction = termination

**STEP 5: Termination**
- **When:** No improvement after final warning, OR gross misconduct
- **Process:** Employment ended with proper notice (unless gross misconduct)
- **Documentation:** Termination letter, final pay calculated

---

**Gross Misconduct = Immediate Termination (Skip Progressive Steps):**

**Serious Offenses That Result in Immediate Termination:**
- Theft (company property, customer property, colleague belongings)
- Fraud, embezzlement, or falsification of documents
- Violence, assault, or physical threats
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Working under influence of alcohol or drugs
- Bringing weapons to workplace
- Sabotage or intentional property destruction
- Revealing confidential information to competitors
- Criminal activity on company premises
- Serious breach of safety causing injury or imminent danger

**Process Even for Gross Misconduct:**
- Immediate suspension (pending investigation)
- Investigation conducted (fair process, hear your side)
- If confirmed: Immediate termination (no notice period required per labor code)
- Police involvement if criminal (theft, assault, etc.)

---

**Your Rights in Discipline Process:**
- Right to know what policy you violated (specific details)
- Right to explain your side of story
- Right to receive copies of all disciplinary documents
- Right to appeal decision (grievance procedure)
- Right to have witness in meeting (colleague, not lawyer)
- Fair and consistent treatment (same rules for everyone)

**What Makes Discipline Fair:**
- Clear rules and expectations (you know what's expected)
- Consistent application (everyone held to same standards)
- Appropriate to offense (punishment fits the crime)
- Progressive (opportunity to improve except gross misconduct)

A: Check your employment contract. Generally permitted if: Doesn't conflict with SS+ schedule, not for a competitor, doesn't use SS+ confidential information or resources, doesn't affect your performance.

---

**Pay & Benefits:**

**Q: What if payday falls on weekend or holiday?**
A: Salary paid on last working day before the 5th.

**Q: How do I check my leave balance?**
A: WAVE PLUS portal shows your balance, or ask Admin Manager anytime.

**Q: When do I get my 13th month salary?**
A: December (with December salary or separately before December 31).

**Q: What if I need a salary advance?**
A: After probation, you can request advance (max 50% of monthly net salary) for emergency. Submit written request to Admin Manager. One advance per 6 months, interest-free, repaid from next paycheck.

---

**Leave:**

**Q: Can I take leave during my first 6 months?**
A: After 6 months employment, you can use accrued leave (~11 days). Before 6 months, only emergency unpaid leave with manager approval.

**Q: What if I'm sick on Saturday?**
A: If you work Saturdays (most employees), same sick leave rules apply - call manager, get medical certificate.

**Q: Do I get paid for public holidays that fall on my rest day?**
A: No - if public holiday falls on Sunday (your normal rest day), no additional compensation. You already have that day off.

---

**Performance:**

**Q: When is my performance review?**
A: Probation reviews at 30, 60, 90 days. Annual review in December for all employees.

**Q: How are salary increases determined?**
A: Two types: (1) Cost of living adjustment (COLA) in January for everyone based on government minimum wage increase, (2) Merit increase in April based on your performance review rating.

**Q: How do I get promoted?**
A: Consistently high performance, develop skills, gain experience, position availability, express your interest to manager. See Section 6.4 for career paths.

---

**Workplace:**

**Q: Can I work from home?**
A: SS+ work generally requires on-site presence (technical work, customer visits, operations). Work from home not typically available except rare circumstances with manager approval.

**Q: What if I need to leave early for personal appointment?**
A: Request manager approval in advance. Make up time if possible or use leave.

**Q: Can I bring my child to work?**
A: Occasionally for very brief period (emergency childcare issue) with manager permission. Not regularly - workplace has hazards and professional environment needed.

---

**Safety:**

**Q: What if I think a task is unsafe?**
A: Stop and tell your Team Leader or manager immediately. You have right to refuse unsafe work. We'll make it safe before proceeding.

**Q: Who pays if I'm injured at work?**
A: CNSS covers medical costs and provides work incapacity benefits (after company declares injury within 48 hours). SS+ may supplement salary during recovery.

**Q: What if customer site requires different PPE than I have?**
A: Tell Team Leader immediately. Company provides any required PPE before you enter site.
