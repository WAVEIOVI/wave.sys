- Work is outside our technical expertise
- We don't have proper equipment or certification
- Safety risks are too high for our team
- Site conditions are unsafe and customer won't address
- Legal/regulatory compliance unclear

**Alternative:** May refer to specialist provider

### Financial Risk

**We Require Deposit or Decline When:**
- Order value is very high relative to customer size/history
- Custom products that cannot be resold
- Long lead time items requiring SS+ to commit funds
- Customer credit limit would be exceeded

### Warning Signs (Red Flags)

**Be Cautious When:**
- Customer is evasive about business details
- Pressure to deliver immediately without proper process
- Requests to bypass normal procedures
- Offers bribes or inappropriate incentives
- Reluctant to sign proper contracts
- History of many supplier changes
- Asks to manipulate invoicing for tax purposes
- Bad reputation in market (check discreetly)

**Process:**
1. Sales rep notes concerns
2. Discusses with Sales Responsible
3. Additional due diligence if needed
4. Decision: Proceed with protections (deposit, limited credit) OR decline
5. Document decision and reasoning

### Saying "No" Professionally

**How to Decline:**
- Thank customer for inquiry
- Be honest but diplomatic: "Unfortunately we cannot accommodate this request at this time"
- If appropriate, suggest alternative: "We can offer these terms instead..."
- Don't criticize or lecture customer
- Don't burn bridges unnecessarily
- Document the decision

**Example Language:**
- "Based on our current capacity and specialization, we're not the best fit for this project. We recommend contacting [specialist] who may be better suited."
- "Our credit policy requires [X] for new customers. We'd be happy to work with you on these terms."
- "For safety reasons and our insurance coverage, we cannot perform this work. However, we can refer you to [specialist]."
