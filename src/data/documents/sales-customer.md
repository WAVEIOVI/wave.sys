## 1.1 LEAD SOURCES & TRACKING

### Where Our Leads Come From

SS+ generates business from multiple channels. Understanding the source helps us prioritize and approach appropriately.

#### Primary Lead Sources

**1. Existing Customer Referrals** ‚≠ê (Highest Quality)
- **Quality:** Very high - pre-qualified and trusted
- **Conversion Rate:** 60-80%
- **Handler:** Assigned to sales rep who manages referring customer
- **Priority:** High - respond within 2 hours
- **Action:** Thank referring customer, follow up with referral immediately

**2. Repeat Customers** ‚≠ê (Highest Value)
- **Quality:** Excellent - already know us
- **Conversion Rate:** 70-90%
- **Handler:** Existing relationship owner
- **Priority:** High - same day response
- **Action:** Review customer history before contact

**3. Website Inquiries** (www.ssplus.tn)
- **Quality:** Medium to High - active interest
- **Conversion Rate:** 30-50%
- **Handler:** Assigned by location (Sfax: Mohamed CHRAITI, Tunis: Kais KHAIRALLAH)
- **Priority:** High - respond within 4 hours
- **Action:** Acknowledge immediately, qualify by phone within 24 hours

**4. Phone Calls**
- **Quality:** Medium - ranges from serious to browsing
- **Conversion Rate:** 25-40%
- **Handler:** Whoever answers ‚Üí qualify ‚Üí assign
- **Priority:** Immediate - handle while on call
- **Action:** Qualify during call, schedule follow-up if needed

**5. Showroom Walk-ins**
- **Quality:** Medium - effort to visit shows interest
- **Conversion Rate:** 30-50%
- **Handler:** Sales rep on duty or available
- **Priority:** Immediate - engage during visit
- **Action:** Product demonstration, needs assessment, same-day quotation if possible

**6. Tenders/RFPs** (Government & Large Companies)
- **Quality:** Variable - competitive process
- **Conversion Rate:** 10-20% (typical for tenders)
- **Handler:** Sales Responsible with Operations Responsible support
- **Priority:** Based on deadline and opportunity size
- **Action:** Bid/No-Bid decision within 24 hours, assign resources

**7. Sales Rep Outreach** (Cold Calling/Visits)
- **Quality:** Low to Medium - not actively looking
- **Conversion Rate:** 5-15%
- **Handler:** Originating sales rep
- **Priority:** Lower - persistent follow-up needed
- **Action:** Build relationship over time, educate on value

### First Contact Goals

Every first contact should achieve:
1. ‚úÖ Make excellent first impression (professional, responsive, helpful)
2. ‚úÖ Understand customer need and urgency
3. ‚úÖ Qualify the opportunity (real need, budget, timeline)
4. ‚úÖ Establish next steps
5. ‚úÖ Begin relationship building

### Customer Qualification (BANT Framework)

Before investing significant time, qualify the lead using **BANT**:

#### **B - BUDGET**
*Questions to ask:*
- "What budget range are you working with for this project?"
- "Is budget already approved, or does this need approval?"
- "Are you looking for a quote to include in a budget proposal?"

*What we're assessing:*
- Do they have financial capacity?
- Is this a real project or just exploring?
- What's the realistic deal size?

#### **A - AUTHORITY**
*Questions to ask:*
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What's your role in the selection process?"
- "Who has final approval authority?"

*What we're assessing:*
- Are we talking to decision maker?
- Who else do we need to reach?
- What's the decision-making process?

#### **N - NEED**
*Questions to ask:*
- "What's driving this need right now?"
- "What problem are you trying to solve?"
- "What happens if you don't address this?"
- "What are you currently using/doing?"

*What we're assessing:*
- Is there a real, urgent need?
- What's the pain point?
- How important is solving this?

#### **T - TIMELINE**
*Questions to ask:*
- "When do you need this in place?"
- "What's driving your timeline?"
- "When are you making a decision?"

*What we're assessing:*
- How urgent is this?
- What's realistic timeline?
- When should we follow up?

### Qualification Outcome

After initial conversation, categorize:

**‚úÖ QUALIFIED** - Has Budget, Authority/Access, Real Need, Clear Timeline 

‚Üí **Action:** Proceed to site survey or quotation preparation

**‚ö†Ô∏è PARTIALLY QUALIFIED** - Missing 1-2 BANT elements but promising

‚Üí **Action:** Continue conversation, gather missing information, nurture

**‚ùå NOT QUALIFIED** - No budget, no authority, no real need, or no timeline

‚Üí **Action:** Polite exit, add to long-term nurture list, minimal investment

## 1.3 SITE SURVEY PROCESS

### When Site Survey is Required

**MANDATORY Site Survey:**
- ‚úÖ New fire equipment installations (extinguishers, hose reels, systems)
- ‚úÖ Annual Maintenance Contracts (first time customers)
- ‚úÖ Complex projects involving multiple product types
- ‚úÖ Large PPE/workwear orders requiring sizing and assessment
- ‚úÖ Custom safety signage projects (layout planning needed)
- ‚úÖ Customer requests site visit

**Site Survey NOT Required (Can Quote Remotely):**
- ‚ùå Repeat customers with established requirements
- ‚ùå Simple product sales (standard items, clear specifications)
- ‚ùå Small orders (under 1,000 TND) with clear specifications
- ‚ùå Remote locations where site visit cost is prohibitive vs. order value

**Exception for Remote/Difficult Locations:**
- Desert locations, offshore platforms, very remote sites
- **Alternative:** Request customer to provide:
  * Detailed photos of facility
  * Floor plans if available
  * Current equipment inventory list
  * Video walkthrough (using phone)
- May require travel fee in quotation or customer acceptance of "best effort" quote

A proper site survey accomplishes:

1. **Hazard Identification & Risk Assessment** (if customer requests)
   - Identify fire hazards and risks
   - Assess current safety measures
   - Evaluate compliance gaps
   - Recommend improvements

2. **Equipment Assessment**
   - Count existing fire equipment (extinguishers, hose reels, etc.)
   - Document types, sizes, locations
   - Assess condition and compliance status
   - Identify equipment needing replacement

3. **Facility Understanding**
   - Facility size and layout
   - Number of buildings/floors
   - Access points and restrictions
   - Work environment (industrial, office, warehouse, etc.)

4. **Employee & PPE Assessment** (for workwear/PPE projects)
   - Number of employees by role/department
   - Current workwear and uniform status
   - Quality and condition of existing PPE
   - Sizing requirements
   - Special requirements (flame-resistant, high-visibility, etc.)

5. **Installation Planning**
   - Mounting locations for new equipment
   - Signage locations and types needed
   - Installation access and logistics
   - Customer site rules and requirements

6. **Relationship Building**
   - Meet key stakeholders (safety manager, facility manager, procurement)
   - Understand customer culture and priorities
   - Demonstrate expertise and professionalism
   - Build trust and confidence

**Arrival (First 5 minutes):**
- Arrive on time (or call if delayed)
- Check in with reception/security per site rules
- Greet contact person warmly and professionally
- Thank them for their time
- Explain purpose and how long survey will take (typically 30-90 minutes)

**Introduction & Discussion (10-15 minutes):**
- Ask about their business and what they do
- Understand their current safety challenges
- Review their specific requirements for this project
- Ask about decision timeline and process
- Discuss their budget parameters (if they're comfortable sharing)

**Physical Survey (30-60 minutes):**

*Use Site Survey Checklist (Appendix B) to document:*

**For Fire Equipment Projects:**
- Walk entire facility systematically (floor by floor, section by section)
- Count and photograph existing fire extinguishers
  * Type (powder, CO2, foam, water, etc.)
  * Size (kg)
  * Location
  * Condition (good, needs service, expired, missing parts)
  * Last service date (check tags)
  * Compliance issues (wrong type for hazard, obstructed, no signage)
- Note areas without coverage (need new equipment)
- Document fire hose reels, blankets, detection systems
- Identify fire hazards and recommend equipment types
- Measure distances for compliance (travel distance to extinguisher)
- Note access issues for installation or service

**For PPE/Workwear Projects:**
- Meet with different departments/teams
- Observe current workwear and PPE in use
- Document number of employees by category
- Note sizing requirements (small, medium, large, XL, etc.)
- Understand work conditions (heat, chemicals, outdoor, etc.)
- Identify specific PPE needs by hazard exposure
- Discuss uniform customization requirements (logos, colors)
- Review replacement/laundering frequency needs

**For Signage Projects:**
- Identify all locations needing signs
- Document sign types needed (exit, warning, mandatory, prohibition)
- Measure mounting surfaces
- Note material requirements (indoor/outdoor, lighting conditions)
- Check for compliance requirements (size, height, colors)

**Throughout Survey:**
- ‚úÖ Take comprehensive photos (wide shots and detail shots)
- ‚úÖ Take notes or use WAVE PLUS to record observations
- ‚úÖ Ask questions when unclear
- ‚úÖ Point out safety concerns you observe
- ‚úÖ Be respectful of customer operations (don't disrupt work)
- ‚úÖ Follow all customer site safety rules

**Closing Discussion (10-15 minutes):**
- Summarize findings
- Discuss recommendations
- Confirm scope of quotation
- Set expectations for quotation delivery (within 1-2 days)
- Ask if they have questions
- Explain next steps
- Thank them for their time
- Exchange business cards/contact info

**After Leaving Site:**
- Complete any missing details in checklist while fresh
- Note any special requirements or commitments made
- Update WAVE PLUS with survey results
- Plan quotation preparation

### Site Survey Best Practices

**DO:**
- ‚úÖ Be thorough but efficient
- ‚úÖ Ask intelligent questions that show expertise
- ‚úÖ Point out safety risks and compliance gaps professionally
- ‚úÖ Listen more than you talk
- ‚úÖ Build rapport with all people you meet
- ‚úÖ Respect customer time and operations
- ‚úÖ Document everything (photos, measurements, notes)
- ‚úÖ Be honest about capabilities and timelines

**DON'T:**
- ‚ùå Rush through the survey
- ‚ùå Criticize their current situation harshly
- ‚ùå Make promises you can't keep
- ‚ùå Quote prices on the spot (unless very simple and standard)
- ‚ùå Badmouth competitors
- ‚ùå Take calls or be distracted during survey
- ‚ùå Violate site safety or security rules
- ‚ùå Forget to thank them

## 1.6 OVERCOMING COMMON OBJECTIONS

### Understanding Objections

Objections are normal and healthy parts of the sales process. They indicate customer is engaged and thinking seriously about the decision.

**Types of Objections:**
- **Price** - "Too expensive"
- **Value** - "Don't see the need" or "Don't see the value"
- **Timing** - "Not right now"
- **Competition** - "Your competitor is cheaper"
- **Trust** - "Don't know your company"
- **Authority** - "Need to check with others"

### The Objection Handling Framework

**Step 1: LISTEN**
- Let customer fully express concern
- Don't interrupt
- Show empathy

**Step 2: ACKNOWLEDGE**
- Validate their concern
- Show understanding
- "I understand your concern about..."

**Step 3: CLARIFY**
- Ask questions to understand the real issue
- "Can you tell me more about...?"
- Often the stated objection isn't the real one

**Step 4: RESPOND**
- Address the specific concern
- Use facts, examples, testimonials
- Reframe perspective

**Step 5: CONFIRM**
- "Does that address your concern?"
- "What else is important to you?"

**Step 6: MOVE FORWARD**
- Return to closing conversation
- "So if we can resolve [concern], are you ready to move forward?"

### Common Objection #1: "Your Price is Too High"

**What They Really Mean:**
- I don't see the value
- I got a cheaper quote elsewhere
- I need to negotiate
- I don't have the budget
- I'm not the decision maker (need ammunition for my boss)

**How to Respond:**

**Technique 1: Value Justification**

*"I understand price is important. Let me explain what makes our pricing different...*

*At SS+, our pricing reflects:*
- *23 years of proven expertise and reliability*
- *Certified technicians - not just anyone can work on life-safety equipment*
- *100% certified products and genuine spare parts - we comply with Tunisian and international standards*
- *06-month warranty on all service work*
- *Quick response times - including 24-hour emergency service*
- *Proper documentation and compliance certificates*
- *Two locations (Sfax and Tunis) for better coverage*

*When it comes to fire safety, you're not just buying a service - you're buying protection for your people and assets. The cheapest option could cost you much more if equipment fails when you need it most.*

*What's most important to you: lowest price or best value and reliability?"*

**Technique 2: Cost of Failure**

*"I appreciate budget concerns. But let me ask you: what's the cost if:*
- *A fire extinguisher doesn't work in an emergency?*
- *Your insurance claim is denied because equipment wasn't properly certified?*
- *An employee is injured due to faulty or non-compliant safety equipment?*
- *You fail a safety inspection and face fines or operational shutdown?*

*The difference between our price and the cheapest option is usually [X TND]. Is that difference worth the risk?"*

**Technique 3: Break Down the Price**

*"Let's look at the actual cost. For your Annual Maintenance Contract:*
- *Total: 3,000 TND per year*
- *That's 250 TND per month*
- *For 25 extinguishers, that's 10 TND per extinguisher per month*
- *Less than 0.35 TND per day per extinguisher*

*For less than the cost of a coffee per day, you have complete peace of mind that all your fire safety equipment is professionally maintained and compliant. Does that seem unreasonable?"*

**Technique 4: Compare Apples to Apples**

*"I'd be happy to review the other quotation with you to ensure you're comparing the same thing. Often cheaper quotes:*
- *Use non-certified refilling materials*
- *Don't include proper documentation*
- *Have less frequent service visits*
- *Don't include genuine spare parts*
- *Have no warranty or support*
- *Aren't compliant with regulations*

*May I ask what's included in the other quote?"*

**Technique 5: Offer Options**

*"I understand budget constraints. Let me offer some options:*
- *We could phase the project over 2-3 months to spread the cost*
- *We could start with the most critical equipment and add more later*
- *For a longer commitment (3-5 year contract), I can offer better pricing*
- *We have payment plans available for larger projects*

*Which approach would work better for you?"*

**When to Discount:**
- Large volume or multi-year commitment
- Can genuinely adjust scope to reduce cost
- Strategic customer worth winning
- Meeting legitimate competitor pricing (within 15% authority, above needs approval)

**When NOT to Discount:**
- Customer just trying to negotiate with no real alternative
- Would set bad precedent for future business
- Already at minimum margin
- Discount would require compromising quality or compliance

**If Customer Still Says No on Price:**
- "I understand. We may not be the right fit for this project. Please keep us in mind for the future."
- "Our pricing reflects our commitment to quality and compliance. If you decide the other route isn't working out, we're here."
- Stay professional and friendly - they may return when cheap option fails

### Common Objection #2: "We Don't See the Value in Regular Maintenance"

**What They Really Mean:**
- Never had a fire, so seems unnecessary
- Don't understand regulations or compliance requirements
- Trying to cut costs
- Had service before but didn't see benefit

**How to Respond:**

**Technique 1: Legal/Regulatory Requirement**

*"I appreciate your question. Regular fire equipment maintenance isn't optional - it's required by Tunisian law and fire safety regulations.*

*Specifically:*
- *Fire extinguishers must be inspected annually minimum (every 6 months recommended)*
- *Hydrostatic testing is required every [X] years*
- *Equipment must have current service tags and documentation*
- *Insurance companies require proof of maintenance*

*If there's an incident and your equipment hasn't been maintained:*
- *Insurance may deny claims*
- *You could face regulatory fines*
- *Personal liability for management*
- *Criminal charges if someone is injured*

*Is compliance and protection worth more than [price] per year?"*

**Technique 2: Insurance Requirement**

*"Has your insurance company asked for proof of fire equipment maintenance? Most commercial policies require:*
- *Annual inspection certificates*
- *Maintenance records*
- *Compliance documentation*

*Without proper maintenance records, your insurance claim could be denied if there's a fire. We've seen this happen. Would you like me to prepare a maintenance schedule that satisfies your insurance requirements?"*

**Technique 3: Equipment Reliability**

*"Fire extinguishers are like car tires - they look fine until you need them in an emergency.*

*Without regular maintenance:*
- *Pressure can drop (extinguisher won't work)*
- *Seals can degrade (contents leak out)*
- *Contents can clump or separate (won't discharge properly)*
- *Valves can corrode (won't operate)*
- *Hoses can crack and fail*

*When there's a fire, there's no second chance. Maintenance ensures equipment works when lives depend on it.*

*Have you ever tested your extinguishers to see if they actually work?"*

**Technique 4: Cost Comparison**

*"Let's compare the costs:*
- *Annual maintenance: 2,500 TND*
- *Replacing all extinguishers because they weren't maintained: 15,000 TND*
- *Equipment failure during a fire: Potentially millions in damage, plus lives at risk*

*Maintenance is insurance against much larger costs. Which makes more business sense?"*

**Technique 5: Risk to People**

*"Beyond regulations and costs, this is about protecting your people. You have [X] employees who come to work trusting that you've provided a safe environment.*

*If there's a fire and the equipment doesn't work because it wasn't maintained, how would you feel? How would their families feel?*

*Fire safety is one area where cutting corners isn't worth it. Can we schedule your first inspection?"*

# SECTION 2: CUSTOMER AGREEMENTS & PRICE LISTS

## 2.1 AGREEMENT TYPES & STRUCTURE

### Agreement Framework Overview

SS+ utilizes a two-tier agreement structure to establish clear expectations and protect both parties:

#### Master Service Agreement (MSA)

**Purpose:** Establishes general terms for ongoing relationships

**Scope:** Long-term service arrangements and general commercial terms

**Duration:** Typically 1-5 years with renewal options

**Use Case:** Key accounts, recurring services, strategic partnerships

#### Service Level Agreement (SLA)

**Purpose:** Defines specific service expectations for individual orders

**Scope:** Particular project or service delivery requirements

**Duration:** Project-specific or service period

**Use Case:** ALL orders (mandatory requirement regardless of MSA status)

### MSA vs SLA Decision Matrix

| Factor | MSA Recommended | SLA Only |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Order Value | >10,000 TND annually | <10,000 TND |
| Relationship Type | Strategic/Long-term | Transactional |
| Service Frequency | Recurring/Ongoing | One-time/Periodic |
| Customer Type | Key accounts, government | Standard customers |
| Complexity | High (multiple services) | Low to medium |
| Investment Level | Significant | Moderate |

**Tunisian Business Law Compliance:**
- All agreements governed by Tunisian commercial law
- Arabic and French language provisions as required
- Local jurisdiction and dispute resolution
- Tax compliance and VAT treatment
- Labor law considerations for service delivery

**Standard Legal Protections:**

- Confidentiality agreements

# SECTION 3: POST-QUOTATION PROCESS

## 3.1 OVERVIEW OF THE PROCESS

After a quotation is sent, the post-quotation process ensures we capture customer confirmation formally, create work orders correctly, and prepare SLAs before execution. **Steps in order:** (1) Obtain confirmation via PO, signed quotation, email, or (in limited cases) verbal with written follow-up; (2) Verify and record confirmation in WAVE PLUS; (3) Create work order with scope, resources, and logistics; (4) Prepare and sign SLA; (5) Proceed to delivery. All confirmation documents must be scanned and attached to the customer record before a work order can be generated.

## 3.2 CLIENT CONFIRMATION METHODS

### Formal Confirmation Hierarchy

SS+ accepts customer confirmation of quotations through multiple channels, with formal documentation requirements varying by method and customer type.

#### Purchase Order (PO) - Preferred Method
**Requirements:**
- Must reference quotation number explicitly
- Include authorized signature and title
- Specify exact products/services ordered
- Include delivery location and timeline
- Provide billing information and payment terms
- Include PO number for invoicing reference

**Processing Protocol:**
1. Verify PO matches quotation exactly
2. Document and resolve any discrepancies before proceeding
3. Enter all PO details in WAVE PLUS system
4. Proceed to work order creation

#### Signed Quotation - Acceptable Alternative
**Requirements:**
- Must be signed by authorized representative
- Include date of signature
- Note any special conditions or changes
- Return complete document with all pages

**Processing Protocol:**
1. Verify signature authority against known contacts
2. Enter acceptance details in WAVE PLUS
3. Attach signed copy to customer record
4. Proceed to work order creation

#### Email Confirmation - Minimum Requirement
**Requirements:**
- Must come from authorized representative
- Explicitly reference quotation number
- Clearly state acceptance of terms
- Include any special instructions or changes
- Save in customer communication record

**Processing Protocol:**
1. Reply acknowledging receipt of confirmation
2. Forward to order processing with clear instructions
3. Enter confirmation details in WAVE PLUS
4. Attach email copy to customer record
5. Proceed to work order creation

#### Verbal Confirmation - Limited Application
**Requirements:**
- Limited to existing customers with established history
- Orders under 5,000 TND only
- From known authorized representative
- Document thoroughly in call notes
- Must be followed by written confirmation within 48 hours
- Include name, title, and time of verbal approval

**Processing Protocol:**
1. Document call details comprehensively
2. Send confirmation email to customer
3. Request written confirmation explicitly
4. Enter details in WAVE PLUS with "Verbal Pending Written" status
5. Follow up if written confirmation not received within 48 hours

### Customer Confirmation Tracking

**System Requirements:**
- All confirmations recorded in WAVE PLUS
- Reference to original quotation mandatory
- Status tracking from confirmation to invoice
- Automated reminders for pending written confirmations
- Escalation alerts for overdue confirmations
